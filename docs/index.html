Toyota Way's 14 principles

Pillar 1: Continuous Improvement (Kaizen)

  • 1. Base your management decisions on a long-term philosophy, even at the expense of short-term financial goals
  • 2. Create continuous process flow to bring problems to the surface
  • 3. Use "pull" systems to avoid overproduction
  • 4. Level out the workload (heijunka)

Toyota Way's 14 principles

Pillar 1: Continuous Improvement (Kaizen)

  • 5. Build a culture of stopping to fix problems, to get quality right the first time
  • 6. Standardized tasks are the foundation for continuous improvement and employee empowerment
  • 7. Use visual control so no problems are hidden
  • 8. Use only reliable, thoroughly tested technology that serves your people and processes

Toyota Way's 14 principles

Pillar 2: Respect for People

  • 9. Grow leaders who thoroughly understand the work, live the philosophy, and teach it to others
  • 10. Develop exceptional people and teams who follow your company's philosophy
  • 11. Respect your extended network of partners and suppliers by challenging them and helping them improve

Toyota Way's 14 principles

Pillar 2: Respect for People

Foundation of Lean manufacturing and influence on Agile/Lean Software Development

Deming's 14 points

Points 1-3: Foundation and Philosophy

  • 1. Create constancy of purpose for improvement of product and service
  • 2. Adopt the new philosophy - reject defective work and poor service
  • 3. Cease dependence on inspection to achieve quality

Deming's 14 points

Points 4-6: Supplier Relations and Continuous Improvement

  • 4. End the practice of awarding business on the basis of price tag alone
  • 5. Improve constantly and forever the system of production and service
  • 6. Institute training on the job

Deming's 14 points

Points 7-9: Leadership and Teamwork

  • 7. Institute leadership - help people and machines do a better job
  • 8. Drive out fear - create an environment where people feel secure
  • 9. Break down barriers between staff areas

Deming's 14 points

Points 10-12: Eliminate Counterproductive Practices

  • 10. Eliminate slogans, exhortations, and targets for the workforce
  • 11. Eliminate numerical quotas for the workforce and numerical goals for management
  • 12. Remove barriers that rob people of pride of workmanship

Deming's 14 points

Points 13-14: Education and Transformation

  • 13. Institute a vigorous program of education and self-improvement
  • 14. Put everybody in the company to work to accomplish the transformation

Deming - Key Concepts

  • Systems Thinking: Most problems are system problems, not people problems
  • Statistical Thinking: Use data and measurement to drive decisions
  • Continuous Improvement: Never-ending cycle of improvement
  • Customer Focus: Understanding and meeting customer needs
  • Employee Involvement: Engaging everyone in the quality process

Lean Software principles

Principles 1-3: Foundation and Value

  • 1. Eliminate Waste: Remove activities that don't add value to the customer (unnecessary features, delays, defects)
  • 2. Amplify Learning: Encourage continuous feedback and iterative development to adapt and improve
  • 3. Decide as Late as Possible: Delay decisions until the last responsible moment to make informed choices

Lean Software principles

Principles 4-6: Delivery and Empowerment

  • 4. Deliver as Fast as Possible: Implement rapid development cycles to meet market demands promptly
  • 5. Empower the Team: Foster trust and autonomy, allowing teams to make decisions and take ownership
  • 6. Build Integrity In: Ensure system reliability and maintainability by integrating quality into every step

Lean Software principles

Principle 7: Systems Thinking

  • 7. See the Whole: Understand the entire system and component interactions to avoid sub-optimization

Focus on value, flow, and people to enhance efficiency, quality, and customer satisfaction

Lean Software

  • Value Stream: Map the flow of value from concept to customer
  • Pull Systems: Let customer demand drive development
  • Continuous Flow: Minimize interruptions and handoffs
  • Perfection: Continuous improvement toward ideal state
  • Respect for People: Trust and empower team members

Extreme Programming

XP Values

  • Communication: Promote face-to-face communication and collaboration
  • Simplicity: Do the simplest thing that could possibly work
  • Feedback: Get feedback early and often from customers and the system
  • Courage: Have the courage to make changes and admit mistakes
  • Respect: Respect for team members, customers, and the work being done

Extreme Programming

Planning Practices

  • Planning Game: Business and development work together to plan releases and iterations
  • Small Releases: Release software frequently in small increments

Extreme Programming

Managing Practices

  • Metaphor: Use a shared story to guide development
  • Sustainable Pace: Work at a pace that can be maintained indefinitely

Extreme Programming

Designing Practices

  • Simple Design: Keep the design as simple as possible
  • Refactoring: Continuously improve the design of the code

Extreme Programming

Coding Practices

  • Pair Programming: Two programmers work together at one computer
  • Collective Code Ownership: Anyone can change any code at any time
  • Coding Standards: Follow consistent coding conventions

Extreme Programming

Testing Practices

  • Test-Driven Development (TDD): Write tests before writing code
  • Continuous Integration: Integrate and test changes frequently

Extreme Programming

Additional Practice

  • On-Site Customer: Have a real customer available to answer questions

Extreme Programming

Key Principles

  • Embrace Change: Welcome changing requirements, even late in development
  • Working Software: Deliver working software frequently
  • Customer Collaboration: Work closely with customers throughout the project
  • Responding to Change: Respond to change over following a plan
